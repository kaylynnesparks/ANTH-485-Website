Archaeology of PKOLS

Mount Douglas is located is Saanich territory, and its traditional name is PKOLS. The mountain has a lot of value for Coast Salish First Nations because it was used as a meeting place between nations.

According to the Mount Douglas Park's newsletter from 2009, three stone wedges were found on the beach. This newsletter also mentions shell midden that was found at the mouth of Douglas creek, and a burial site of a single, unidentified body that was later reburied in an unidentified site. Very little information can be found about any of these archaeological finds and I can't find any confirmation that any of it came from Coast Salish peoples.
